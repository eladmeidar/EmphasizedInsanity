<a target="_blank" href="http://www.google.com/analytics/"><strong>Google Analytics</strong></a> has a new feature that <strong>Ajax &amp; Flash</strong> developers will find extremely useful . According to the new article, is now possible to <strong>track page views in Ajax &amp; Flash</strong> applications. And, it is simple.<br /><br /><code><br />http_request.onreadystatechange = sendAlert;<br />http_request.open(’GET’, url, true);<br />http_request.send(null);<br />}<br /><br />function sendAlert() {<br /> if (http_request.readyState == 4) {<br /> if (http_request.status == 200) {<br />    alert(http_request.responseText);<br />    pageTracker._trackPageview("/pagefilename1" ); }<br /> else {<br />    alert(’Error.’);<br /> }<br />}<br />}<br /></code><br />An important usage tip about this feature is that, <strong>Google Analytics</strong> codes are normally installed just before the /body tag. When calling <strong>_trackPageview</strong> function, you’ll need to use it after the Analytics codes are included. Which means installing the Analytics codes just after the tag is a guaranteed solution.
